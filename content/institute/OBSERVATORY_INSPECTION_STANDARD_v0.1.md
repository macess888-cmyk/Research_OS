# PURPOSE

The Observatory Inspection Standard (OIS) defines the methodology used by the Observatory Institute to conduct independent architectural inspections.

Its purpose is to improve understanding through disciplined observation, careful distinction, structured comparison, and constructive dialogue.

The standard applies equally to:

- Research frameworks
- Software architectures
- Scientific methodologies
- Governance proposals
- Institutional practices
- Observatory projects
- The Observatory itself

---

# FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLE

The purpose of an inspection is not to determine ownership, authority, or final truth.

Its purpose is to improve understanding.

---

# OBSERVATIONAL INTEGRITY

The Observatory distinguishes:

Observation ≠ Interpretation

Interpretation ≠ Validation

Validation ≠ Authority

Documentation ≠ Truth

Agreement ≠ Evidence

Evidence earns revision.

Revision earns confidence.

Confidence never replaces continued observation.

---

# INSPECTION POSTURE

Every inspection should remain:

Independent

Evidence-oriented

Constructive

Respectful

Transparent

Reproducible

Repeatable

Implementation-independent

---

# THE OBSERVATORY DOES NOT

The Observatory does not:

Certify

Approve

Reject

Endorse

Govern

Own

Replace expert judgment

The Observatory observes, documents, distinguishes, and questions.

Every inspection follows the same progression.

↓

# STANDARD INSPECTION LAYERS

Every inspection attempts to distinguish:

## Purpose

What problem is being addressed?

---

## Foundations

Which concepts are fundamental?

Which are derived?

---

## Architecture

How are responsibilities separated?

What relationships exist?

---

## Implementation

How are architectural concepts realized?

---

## Evidence

Which claims are supported?

Which remain hypotheses?

---

## Unknowns

Which questions remain unresolved?

---

## Future Investigation

What deserves continued research?

# UNKNOWN REGISTER

Every inspection maintains an Unknown Register.

Possible categories include:

Requires Evidence

Requires Clarification

Requires Replication

Requires Counterexample

Requires Comparison

Requires Investigation

Unknowns are preserved.

Unknowns are never hidden.

---

# DISTINGUISHING WRITING

The Observatory distinguishes three kinds of writing.

## Observations

Describe only what has been observed.

---

## Reflections

Describe possible interpretations.

Clearly distinguish these from observations.

---

## Recommendations

Suggest possible future work.

Recommendations remain optional.

---

# COMPARATIVE INSPECTION

When comparing multiple frameworks:

Identify similarities.

Identify distinctions.

Preserve independent contributions.

Avoid unnecessary convergence.

Independent work may be complementary without being identical.

---

# GUIDING PRINCIPLE

Every inspection should leave the inspected work clearer than it was found.

The objective is not to determine who is correct.

The objective is to strengthen understanding.

---

# CLOSING STATEMENT

Independent inspection is an act of stewardship.

The Observatory exists to preserve observations, distinguish assumptions from evidence, encourage respectful dialogue, and support continual refinement through disciplined inquiry.

Every inspection is an opportunity to learn.

Every question is an opportunity to improve.

Everything not yet supported by sufficient evidence remains:

UNKNOWN → HOLD.
