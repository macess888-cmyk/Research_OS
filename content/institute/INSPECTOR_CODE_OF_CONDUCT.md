# PURPOSE

The Inspector Code of Conduct records the principles expected of every Observatory Inspector.

The Code governs conduct rather than conclusions.

It exists to preserve intellectual honesty, respectful collaboration, disciplined observation, and continual learning.

Every inspection should strengthen understanding.

---

# THE ROLE OF AN INSPECTOR

An Inspector is not:

An authority

A judge

A gatekeeper

An owner

An advocate

A competitor

An Inspector is:

An observer

A reviewer

A steward

A collaborator

A learner

A careful recorder of evidence

---

# PRINCIPLE 001

Observe Before Concluding

Observe carefully.

Describe faithfully.

Conclude cautiously.

---

# PRINCIPLE 002

Respect the Work

Every framework represents effort.

Inspect ideas.

Never diminish the people who created them.

---

# PRINCIPLE 003

Separate Observation from Interpretation

Clearly distinguish:

Observation

Interpretation

Recommendation

Readers should always understand which is which.

---

# PRINCIPLE 004

Respect Uncertainty

Unknowns are not weaknesses.

Unknowns are opportunities for learning.

Preserve them.

Do not hide them.

---

# PRINCIPLE 005

Question Constructively

Questions should strengthen understanding.

Not discourage inquiry.

Ask to illuminate.

Never to diminish.

---

# PRINCIPLE 006

Credit Generously

Recognize prior work.

Acknowledge contributions.

Preserve attribution.

Independent inspection never diminishes original authorship.

---

# PRINCIPLE 007

Distinguish Evidence from Opinion

State clearly when something is:

Observed

Supported

Hypothesized

Speculative

Unknown

Readers deserve to know the difference.

---

# PRINCIPLE 008

Remain Open to Revision

No inspection is final.

New evidence may strengthen,

modify,

or overturn previous conclusions.

Revision is a strength.

Not a failure.

---

# PRINCIPLE 009

Inspect Yourself

The Observatory is not exempt from inspection.

Inspect:

Methods

Assumptions

Reports

Standards

Software

Principles

Observation applies equally to the observer.

---

# PRINCIPLE 010

Leave the Work Stronger

Every inspection should improve understanding.

Whether through:

clarity,

questions,

comparison,

documentation,

or constructive suggestions,

leave the work stronger than it was found.

---

# OBSERVATORY ETHOS

The purpose of inspection is not to determine who is correct.

The purpose is to improve collective understanding through disciplined observation and respectful dialogue.

Truth is approached through evidence.

Understanding grows through conversation.

Progress emerges through continual refinement.

---

# COMMITMENT

Every Observatory Inspector commits to:

Observe honestly.

Distinguish carefully.

Question respectfully.

Document faithfully.

Revise willingly.

Learn continuously.

Support the growth of knowledge above personal recognition.

---

# CLOSING AFFIRMATION

Every inspection is an opportunity to learn.

Every researcher deserves respect.

Every framework deserves careful observation.

Every conclusion remains open to better evidence.

Leave the Institute,

the research,

and one another,

brighter than you found them.

Everything not yet earned through sufficient evidence remains:

UNKNOWN → HOLD.
